About

I work at the intersection of mathematics, scientific computing, and machine learning, developing methods that make complex, high-dimensional systems tractable, efficient, and transparent. I earned my PhD in Applied Mathematics and Computational Sciences from King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) under the supervision of Prof. Daniele Boffi, and I hold a BSc in Physics with a minor in Mathematics from the University of Washington, Seattle.

My research focuses on modeling and controlling high-dimensional systems under uncertainty. I design computationally efficient surrogate models that replace expensive full-scale simulations while preserving their essential structure and predictive power. By combining adaptive sampling strategies, probabilistic modeling, and low-rank techniques such as tensor-train decompositions, I enable scalable analysis, rigorous uncertainty quantification, and systematic exploration of complex design spaces. These methods support reliable decision-making across engineering, scientific computing, finance, and economics.

Looking ahead, my goal is to translate advanced mathematical theory into explainable, high-impact computational systems, bridging rigorous foundations with practical deployment to address real-world challenges across disciplines.

Research

Key areas spanning probabilistic modeling, decision-making, data compression, and computational methods.

01

Probabilistic Modeling & Machine Learning

Uncertainty-aware prediction and inference using kernel methods and Gaussian processes.

Gaussian-process priors on function spaces (viewed through the reproducing-kernel Hilbert space lens) treat the kernel as the unifying object linking GPs, graphical/state-space models, and wide-network/linearized neural predictors. Emphasis on scalable inference via sparse/Markov and state-space representations, with attention to calibration, robustness to distribution shift, and interpretability.

02

Decision Making in Finance & Economics

Sequential decision making under uncertainty grounded in dynamic programming, stochastic control, and robust optimization.

Emphasis on value functions, policy approximation, and risk-sensitive objectives. Probabilistic uncertainty quantification informs policies for investment, forecasting, and resource allocation, with explicit attention to constraints, transparency and the ability to satisfy domain requirements such as Sharia-compliant finance.

03

Data Compression with Tensor Trains

Low-rank approximation via the tensor-train (TT) decomposition technique.

The focus is on rank-adaptive TT factorizations with perturbation-stable cores and explicit error/complexity control (cost scaling essentially linearly in dimension for bounded ranks). These TT surrogates compress discretized PDE operators and GP covariances, enabling scalable simulation, inference, and uncertainty quantification, and integrate naturally with kernel and probabilistic models.

04

Simplifying Complex Mathematical Models

Parametric PDEs with high-dimensional parameter spaces, formulated at the level of the solution operator and associated spectral maps.

Develop certified reduced models that balance fidelity and cost via parameter-aware adaptive sampling and rigorous a posteriori control, while treating spectral structure (eigenvalue branches and eigenspaces) as a primary output for stable mode tracking and provable complexity/accuracy guarantees.

Publications

Published work and ongoing research projects.

Published

On the Matching of Eigensolutions to Parametric PDEs

M. Alghamdi, F. Bertrand, D. Boffi, F. Bonizzoni, A. Halim, G. Priyadarshi

ECCOMAS Congress, Oslo (2022)

Introduced a similarity-based framework for consistently matching eigensolutions across parameter instances in parametric PDEs, enabling stable tracking of eigenbranches and supporting greedy reduced-order modeling and surrogate construction for high-dimensional spectral problems.

Published

A Greedy Model Order Reduction Method for Parametric Elliptic PDEs

M. Alghamdi, D. Boffi, F. Bonizzoni

Journal of Computational and Applied Mathematics

Develops a certified greedy algorithm for efficiently constructing reduced-order models in high-dimensional parameter domains, with explicit error estimates and complexity guarantees.

Published

Data-Driven Method for Parametric PDE Eigenvalue Problems Using Gaussian Processes

M. Alghamdi, F. Bertrand, D. Boffi, A. Halim

Computational Methods in Applied Mathematics

Proposes a GP-based surrogate modeling framework to approximate eigenvalue trajectories in parametric PDEs, improving accuracy in non-affine, irregular parameter regimes.

In Preparation

Robust and Efficient Tracking of Eigensolutions in High-Dimensional Parameter Domains

M. Alghamdi, D. Boffi, F. Bonizzoni

Scalable algorithms for detecting and following eigensolution branches in very high-dimensional spaces, with emphasis on robustness against parameter irregularities.

In Preparation

Learning-informed greedy algorithms for parametric PDE eigenvalue problems

Extending a certified greedy framework with data-driven parameter selection and advanced surrogate modeling; exploring machine learning methods (e.g., GPR, neural networks) to automate eigensolution classification and improve approximation in high-dimensional, non-affine settings.

In Preparation

Tensor-Train methods for parametric eigenvalue problems

Developing a framework to represent parametric eigenvalue problems in Tensor-Train (TT) format, treating the collection of eigenvectors as a tensor. The project also explores TT-based optimization strategies for non-uniform parameter sampling, as an alternative to sparse grids, to minimize redundant data generation.

In Preparation

Combining Tensor-Train decomposition with Neural Networks and Gaussian Process Regression

Investigating hybrid approaches that leverage TT structure to improve the scalability of Neural Networks and Gaussian Process Regression in high-dimensional parameter spaces.

In Preparation

Symbolic detection of discrete symmetries in evolution equations

Implementing algorithms in Mathematica to enable automated symmetry-based analysis beyond traditional variational or Lie symmetry methods.
